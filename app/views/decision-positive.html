Your decision

Based on the established facts of your appeal, it’s our decision that you are eligible for Personal Independence Payment (PIP) at the following rate:

  • Daily living - Standard rate
  • Mobillity - No award

This is more than the DWP initially decided you were entitled to so you have won your appeal. Back payments of the PIP benefit are due from 12 March 2017 at the above rate.

How we made our decision

We reached the decision by considering all the evidence you and DWP submitted in relation to your appeal. This included the additional evidence you submitted, including:

  • letter from your doctor
  • letter from your carer
  • written statement from yourself

After considering the above evidence we acknowledge that you experience pain in your lower back when doing some tasks around the house. Specifically washing yourself and preparing food. We consider that this pain does not hinder you enough to be awarded the enhanced rate of the daily living component of PIP.

PIP activities and the descriptors we consider apply
Preparing food Points
Needs to use an aid or appliance to be able to prepare or cook a simple meal 2
Cannot cook a simple meal using a conventional cooker but is able to do so using a microwave 2

Washing and bathing Points
Needs to use an aid or appliance to be able to wash or bathe 2
Needs assistance to be able to wash either their hair or body below the waist 2

What happens next

You can accept the decision or if you feel that this decision is wrong, you can request a hearing where you can explain your appeal in person to the tribunal.
