The tribunal’s view on your appeal


The tribunal have considered all the information submitted by you and DWP.

Their view is that you’re entitled to PIP at the rate below from 4 April 2018 until 7 February 2022.

Daily living Standard rate
Mobility Enhanced rate

This is more than DWP initially awarded you.

If both you and DWP accept this view, you’ll be awarded PIP at the above rate.

The reasons for the tribunal’s view

We considered all the evidence you and DWP submitted in relation to your appeal.

We agreed that your ability to walk is more limited than in the decision under appeal. This is because of a combination of your back pain, obesity and heart problems. For this reason we have increased the award for the mobility component to the enhanced rate.

We also agree that you have greater difficulties getting in and out of the bath. Accordingly we have increased the points awarded for this activity, but this does not affect the level of the award of this component.

PIP activities and the descriptors we consider apply to your appeal
Activity Descriptor Points
Preparing food Needs to use an aid or appliance to be able to prepare or cook a simple meal 2
Washing and bathing Needs assistance to be able to get in or out of a bath or shower 3
Managing toilet needs or incontinence Needs to use an aid or appliance to be able to manage my toilet needs or incontinence 2
Dressing and undressing Needs to use an aid or appliance to be able to dress or undress 2
Moving around Can stand and then move more than 1 metre but no more than 20 metres either aided or unaided 12

What happens next

You can accept the view or if you feel the view is wrong you can have a face to face hearing.


Do you accept this view?

You have to respond to this question by 2 May 2018. If you don’t respond we will assume you are accepting the view and it will be sent to you.
