Answering the tribunal’s questions

The tribunal need some additional information from you to help them make a decision on your appeal. The information you provide will be treated as evidence on your appeal.

Your representative has been sent access to your online appeal so they can log in and answer the question for you. What they write is treated as evidence so it’s important that you know what they are submitting. Ask them to save their answer and you can check it before they submit it.

Deadline to answer the questions

You are given a deadline with which you have to answer the questions. This is usually a week but may be longer if the tribunal ask you to provide some additional evidence, such as a doctor’s letter.

If you need more time then you can extend the deadline. You will be given an extra week automatically. You’ll need to give reasons if you need longer than that.

How do you want to provide additional evidence?

Providing evidence is optional. Only send or upload evidence which shows how your condition affects your life at the time of the decision by DWP.
